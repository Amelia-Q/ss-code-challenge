What's All This About?

There were 3.6 billion people in the world in 1970, 50 years later in 2020, the population has more than doubled to over 7.5 billion. It would be impossible for this big change to not have had an impact on the environment as a whole, given the industrial revolution(s). We are beginning to reckon with the consequences as the ramifications of the climate crisis are felt, from heat waves and hurri- canes to species extinction and habitat loss. We all need to have a better understanding of how the global population is impacting the climate crisis, how urban areas are being changed by human choices, and therefore what kind of world we are likely to see in another 50 years when the world population has continued to grow or at least plateaued at this high level. Without changes now, future generations may be entering a world that is quickly becoming uninhabitable to them.
